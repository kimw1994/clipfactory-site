## Example 1: B2B Buyer-Story Podcast

Use case: a podcast interviews software buyers about how they evaluated tools, handled risk, and decided what to buy.

| Clip Angle | Hook | Title | Platform Fit | Why It Works |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Buyer pain in their own words | The best buyer clip starts where the buyer names the pain without the host translating it. | Start With The Buyer's Words | LinkedIn | Makes the market problem immediately clear to operators and sellers. |
| Vendor credibility moment | You can often hear the sale happen before the contract, when the buyer explains why a vendor felt credible. | The Credibility Signal | LinkedIn | Useful for founders and GTM teams improving buyer trust. |
| Deal-breaker moment | The strongest short may be the reason a buyer almost said no. | The Deal-Breaker Clip | LinkedIn / Shorts | Objection-led clips create sharper discussion than success-story clips. |
| Internal stakeholder objection | A buyer journey becomes useful when you hear the internal objection they had to overcome. | The Hidden Stakeholder | LinkedIn | Maps directly to B2B sales conversations and buying committees. |
| GTM lesson | The clip worth saving is the one a sales team can use before the next call. | From Story To Sales Lesson | LinkedIn | Converts buyer-story content into practical operating insight. |

## Example 2: SaaS Founder Interview

Use case: a founder interview covers pricing, growth, retention, distribution, hiring, mistakes, and company strategy.

| Clip Angle | Hook | Title | Platform Fit | Why It Works |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Pricing decision | The pricing decision usually explains the business better than the revenue headline. | The Pricing Choice | LinkedIn | Gives operators a concrete decision to debate or apply. |
| Distribution channel | The useful clip is not "we grew fast"; it is the channel that actually worked and why. | The Channel That Worked | LinkedIn / Shorts | Strong standalone lesson for founders and marketers. |
| Retention lesson | Churn stories are often more useful than growth stories because they show what customers actually valued. | What Kept Customers | LinkedIn | Buyer relevance is higher than broad inspiration. |
| Founder mistake | The mistake the founder would not repeat is usually the clip people remember. | The Mistake Clip | TikTok / Reels | High-tension, easy to understand, and naturally human. |
| Constraint-driven strategy | A sharper strategy often starts with a constraint the founder could not ignore. | The Constraint That Focused The Company | LinkedIn | Shows decision quality, not just company size. |

## Example 3: B2B AI Founder Story

Use case: an interview covers product-led growth, enterprise adoption, AI workflow, user trust, and strategic discipline.

| Clip Angle | Hook | Title | Platform Fit | Why It Works |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Product-led growth argument | What if the product, not the content calendar, did the marketing? | Product As Marketing Channel | LinkedIn | Clear founder/operator tension around growth strategy. |
| Bottom-up enterprise adoption | The user who adopts first is not always the buyer who signs later. | Bottom-Up Before Top-Down | LinkedIn | Strong for B2B SaaS and enterprise GTM discussion. |
| Data network effect | The moat starts when every user makes the system better for the next one. | The Data Moat | YouTube Shorts / LinkedIn | Turns an AI concept into a concise business lesson. |
| Quality over vanity growth | Four million users only matters if the right users are getting value. | Growth That Matters | LinkedIn | Avoids vanity metric framing and adds strategic nuance. |
| Founder discipline | The growth story is less useful than the constraint behind the growth. | The Discipline Behind Growth | LinkedIn | Founder audiences respond to decision-making, not just outcomes. |
