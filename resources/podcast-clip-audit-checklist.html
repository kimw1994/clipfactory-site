Selection checklist

Use this before cutting a podcast, webinar, or founder interview.

The goal is not to find the loudest quote. The goal is to find moments that a new viewer can understand and that a qualified prospect would care about.

01

Start after the setup.

Look for the first sentence that creates tension, names a mistake, or gives a clear claim.

02

Test standalone clarity.

The clip should make sense without the host intro, full backstory, or a private inside reference.

03

Prefer buyer decisions.

Prioritize objections, comparisons, implementation lessons, risk reduction, and criteria for choosing.

04

Keep claims supportable.

Do not add revenue, client, medical, legal, financial, or performance claims that the speaker did not make.

05

Match the platform.

LinkedIn favors authority and practical lessons. Shorts and Reels need faster context and cleaner visual pacing.

06

Write the editor note.

Capture where to start, what to remove, what to subtitle, and what must stay inside mobile safe margins.

Platform fit

The same episode needs different opening logic by channel.

LinkedIn

Authority and buyer education.

Use clips that expose a decision, mistake, framework, or market point of view.

Shorts

Searchable teaser value.

Use one complete idea that can send interested viewers back to the full episode.

TikTok

Fast context and contrast.

Open on the surprising statement, then add only enough context for the lesson to land.

Reels

Visual pacing and caption clarity.

Use moments that can survive silent viewing and tight mobile UI safe margins.
