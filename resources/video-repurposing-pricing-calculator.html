Decision rules

Do not buy volume before the first episode proves the pattern.

The main risk is paying for more editing before the clip selection logic is clear. A written audit keeps the first step small, inspectable, and easy to hand to an editor.

If the content format is unproven
Start with the $149 Clip Audit and inspect the clip map before committing to a monthly plan.
If you publish one episode weekly
Use Starter after the first audit shows repeatable clip opportunities.
If you need several platform variants
Use Growth when the issue is volume and platform adaptation, not first-pass strategy.
If claims or compliance matter
Keep an avoid-list. Do not invent claims, results, customer details, or platform promises.

Monthly fit

A monthly plan is useful only when the source content is already recurring.

01

You publish consistently.

Monthly planning works when there is a reliable weekly or biweekly source episode.

02

You can confirm rights.

The client must own or have permission to edit and republish the source content.

03

You know the primary platform.

LinkedIn, YouTube Shorts, TikTok, and Reels each need different opening logic.

04

You have an editor or want one scoped.

The audit is a planning deliverable. Final video editing is separate unless explicitly scoped.

05

You can provide an avoid-list.

Private names, unsupported claims, regulated topics, and customer details should be named before work starts.

06

You want a repeatable content system.

The plan should turn episodes into reusable assets, not random highlights.
