Common questions

Use these answers before asking for a manual quote.

The fastest route is to send one public episode link, choose one primary platform, confirm rights, and list anything that should not appear in clips or captions.

How long is the source episode?
The audit works best for a 20-90 minute podcast, webinar, founder interview, LinkedIn Live, or YouTube episode.
Can one audit cover multiple platforms?
Yes, but the quote should name one primary platform first. LinkedIn, Shorts, TikTok, and Reels need different opening logic.
Do you need raw files?
No for the first scope review. Start with a public link. Raw files are only discussed after the scope is clear.
Can the audit be used by my existing editor?
Yes. The deliverable is written so a human editor can use the timestamp, hook, caption, and cut guidance.
What makes a buyer-ready source?
Clear audio, useful claims, practical lessons, named objections, comparison points, and permission to edit and republish.
When should I choose a monthly plan instead?
Choose monthly only after one episode proves there are repeatable clip opportunities and a recurring publishing cadence.

Buyer handoff

Send enough context to make the quote specific.

01

Public source link.

Use a YouTube, podcast, webinar replay, LinkedIn Live, or public episode page link.

02

Primary platform.

Choose LinkedIn, YouTube Shorts, TikTok, or Instagram Reels so the hook style is not vague.

03

Clip goal.

Name whether the clips should support authority, leads, education, or product awareness.

04

Rights confirmation.

Confirm that you own the content or have permission to process, edit, and republish it.

05

Avoid-list.

Name claims, customer details, regulated topics, private names, or competitor comparisons to avoid.

06

Preferred next step.

Choose the $149 audit first unless there is already a proven recurring episode workflow.
